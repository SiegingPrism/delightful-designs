# User Personas

## P1 — "Maya the Maker" (Primary)
- **Age:** 24, indie developer / creator
- **Context:** Works on her own SaaS by day, posts on X by night. No boss, no structure.
- **Pain:** "I have 12 things I *could* do. I don't know which one matters today. By 4pm I've doomscrolled and shipped nothing."
- **Goal:** A system that tells her what to do next *and* makes finishing feel like a win.
- **Wins with FlowSphere:** AI Coach picks 3 tasks each morning. Pomodoro + XP makes shipping addictive. Streak ≥ 30 days = unlocks a new theme.

## P2 — "Dev the Student" (Secondary)
- **Age:** 19, CS undergrad
- **Context:** Juggling coursework, side projects, gym, sleep deficit.
- **Pain:** Habitica feels like a kids' game. Notion is too much setup. Calendar is empty even when his week is full.
- **Goal:** Build study + workout + sleep streaks without thinking about it.
- **Wins with FlowSphere:** Habit heatmap, mood tracking, "lighter day" suggestions before exams.

## P3 — "Priya the Knowledge Worker" (Tertiary)
- **Age:** 32, senior PM at a remote-first company
- **Context:** Back-to-back Zooms, energy crashes at 3pm, can't separate work from rest.
- **Pain:** Burnout cycles every 6 weeks. Productivity apps make it worse by hiding the cost.
- **Goal:** Sustainable output. Wants the app to *protect* her, not push her.
- **Wins with FlowSphere:** Burnout detection, mood-aware coaching, recovery missions worth XP.

## Anti-persona — "Sam the Manager"
- Wants Gantt charts, team assignments, sprint planning.
- **We are not for Sam.** Tell him to use Linear or Asana.

## Common traits across P1–P3
- Dark mode by default.
- Mobile-first, but use desktop for deep work.
- Allergic to forms and setup wizards longer than 60 seconds.
- Will quit any app that doesn't show value in session 1.
